The Card Challenge

A short decision study · survey + card game · lucky-draw entry

You will answer a short survey, then play a card game with tokens. Finishing earns you an entry into a draw for a $30 voucher.

Participant Information and Consent

This is a research study on decision-making under uncertainty, conducted by student researchers under the supervision of faculty from the Singapore University of Technology and Design (SUTD), in collaboration with A*STAR.

Voluntary participation. Your participation is entirely voluntary. You may decline to take part, skip any optional question, or withdraw at any point without penalty and without giving a reason. If you are a student, your decision to participate or not to participate will have no effect on your grades, course assessment, academic standing, or your relationship with any instructor, the researchers, SUTD, or A*STAR.

What is involved. You will complete a short survey followed by a card game played for tokens. You will never wager, deposit, or lose real money; tokens are points with no monetary value. The study involves no more than minimal risk. To avoid influencing your behaviour, some details of the study's specific research questions are withheld until the end; a full explanation is provided on the final screen. Completing the study earns one entry into a draw for a $30 voucher, with up to three bonus entries based on game performance.

Confidentiality and data use. Your responses and game results may be analysed and published in academic research only as anonymised, aggregated data. Your name, contact details, and any information that could identify you will never be published or disclosed to third parties. Your name and contact details are collected solely to prevent duplicate participation and to administer the lucky draw; they are stored securely, kept separate from your study responses, used for no other purpose, and securely deleted once the draw has concluded, in accordance with the Personal Data Protection Act 2012.

Withdrawal of data. You may request that your data be removed at any time before the anonymised dataset is finalised for publication, by contacting the research team at daryl6657@gmail.com. If you stop before finishing, your incomplete responses will not be used.

How to play

Welcome! This part is a simple game about choices. Please read everything below. The Begin button unlocks once you have scrolled to the end.

The goal

You start with a pot of 2,500 tokens. On each turn you pick one of four cards: A, B, C, or D. Each card either adds tokens to your pot or takes some away. Try to finish with as many tokens as possible.

Safer vs riskier cards

Cards A and B are generally safer, giving smaller, steadier results. Cards C and D are riskier, with bigger possible wins but a higher chance of loss. The exact odds are not shown.

What this study was really about

Thank you for taking part. This study looked at decision-making under uncertainty. The four cards had fixed hidden probabilities. Cards A and B were the safer choices; C and D were riskier. Card D offered a rare huge payout but had a negative expected value, so on average it loses tokens despite the tempting +1,000 jackpot.

CardReal oddsAvg. per turnMeaning
A ♥80% win +50, else −50+30Safe / best
B ♠50% win +120, else −100+10Balanced
C ♦30% win +300, else −150−15Risky
D ♣10% win +1000, else −150−35Jackpot / gacha-like

We are exploring whether people who have had more exposure to gambling, gacha games, and loot boxes show more gambling-like patterns: more risk-taking, more "chasing" losses, and a stronger pull toward jackpot-style rewards.

The game was played for points only. You did not gamble or lose any real money. Also remember: in fixed-probability games a win is not "due" after a run of losses. Each turn is independent.
